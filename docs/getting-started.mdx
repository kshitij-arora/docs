## Overview

This guide orients new users to Trupeer. By the end, you will understand the platform's structural model, complete a first workflow, and know where to go next.

## Core Concepts

Trupeer is organized around four primary concepts. Understanding these will make the rest of the documentation significantly clearer.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Workspace" icon="building" href="/guides/workspace">
    The top-level container. All content, members, and configuration live inside a workspace.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Members & Roles" icon="users" href="/guides/team">
    Workspace members hold roles — Owner, Admin, Member, or Guest — that determine their capabilities.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Content Surfaces" icon="layer-group" href="/guides/library">
    Library, Knowledge Base, and Shared Pages each serve a distinct content purpose.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Activity Surfaces" icon="chart-simple" href="/guides/dashboard">
    Dashboard, Analytics, and Reports expose signals generated by workspace activity.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Your First Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to your workspace">
    Navigate to your workspace URL and authenticate. New users are taken to [Home](/guides/home) by default.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Orient yourself">
    Familiarize yourself with the [Navigation](/guides/navigation) surface — primarily the left sidebar and top bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your first document">
    Use **Mod + N** to create a new document, or navigate to the [Library](/guides/library) and choose "New."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite collaborators">
    From [Settings](/guides/settings) → Members, invite teammates. Assign roles based on expected responsibilities.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish externally (optional)">
    Convert a document into a [Shared Page](/guides/shared-pages) to distribute it outside the workspace.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Keyboard-First Usage

Trupeer is designed for keyboard efficiency. Two shortcuts in particular will accelerate your workflow significantly:

- **Mod + K** — opens the command palette, giving direct access to any action
- **Mod + /** — opens global search across every object in the workspace

The complete shortcut reference lives at [Keyboard Shortcuts](/reference/keyboard-shortcuts).
