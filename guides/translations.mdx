## Overview

Trupeer can translate a finished piece of content into another language in seconds — script, voiceover, and accompanying document all at once. Translation preserves the original edits (trims, skips, pauses, scenes) and replaces only the language-dependent pieces.

## Translate a video

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Translate Content">
    In the [Video Editor](/guides/video-editor), top right → **Translate Content**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the target language">
    65+ languages supported, including major European, Asian, and Latin American languages.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the translation">
    Click **Create Translation**. The video, transcript, and scene captions regenerate in the chosen language.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and refine">
    The translated copy opens as its own version. Edit the script, swap the voice, or regenerate the voiceover as needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Translate a document

In the [Document Editor](/guides/documents), the translation option behaves identically. Title, description, and every content block translate in place. Images are preserved.

## Voice selection per language

Each target language maps to a default Trupeer voice. You can override the default by picking a different voice from the **AI Voice** panel — useful when a specific accent or gender is required.

## Original vs. translated content

- A translation is a **separate draft** linked to the source video; it does not overwrite the original.
- Edits in the source do **not** automatically propagate to translations — re-translate if you change the source script.
- Translations count toward your plan's AI minutes. See [Pricing](/guides/pricing).
