## Overview

The Video Editor is where a generated draft becomes a finished video. It combines a live preview, a timeline, and a tabbed sidebar exposing every feature Trupeer supports — script, chapters, zoom, music, annotations, slides, transitions, avatars, and more.

## The editing loop

<Steps>
  <Step title="Edit the script">
    Refine wording, trim/skip chunks, add pauses. See [Script Editor](/guides/script-editor).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Shape the visuals">
    Background, intro/outro slides, scenes, chapters, aspect ratio, zoom, transitions, captions. See [Visuals](/guides/visuals).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add overlays and blur">
    Text, shapes, images, and blur for sensitive regions. See [Annotations](/guides/annotations).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a voice or avatar">
    Choose an [AI voice](/guides/voices) or toggle an [AI avatar](/guides/avatars).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refresh the voiceover">
    Trupeer only re-renders audio on demand. Click **Refresh VoiceOver** after material script changes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish or export">
    Open [Shared Pages](/guides/shared-pages) to publish with analytics, or export a raw MP4.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Keyboard and gesture tips

- **Spacebar** toggles preview playback.
- Drag the timeline edges of a zoom, annotation, or image to adjust when it appears.
- The timeline's eye icon hides/reveals detailed timeline tracks.
- Changes autosave; explicit **Save** applies them to the draft.

## Drafts and versions

Every generation produces a draft. If a re-generation goes wrong, you can roll back via the version picker in the bottom bar. The last known good draft remains available until you explicitly delete it.
